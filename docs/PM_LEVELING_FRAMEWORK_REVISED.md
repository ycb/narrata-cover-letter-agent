# Narrata PM Leveling Framework

## Four Core Competencies + Scope as a Dimension

### Purpose

Narrata's goal is to provide an objective, evidence-based way to infer a product manager's level and show exactly what to improve.

After synthesizing 30+ public ladders, academic resources, and practitioner insights (Meta, Google, Intercom, Wise, Reforge, Lenny Rachitsky, progression.fyi), we found strong consensus around four enduring competencies that define PM effectiveness—regardless of company size or industry.

### The Four Competencies

| Competency | What it Measures | Observable Evidence |
|------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Execution** | The ability to reliably deliver outcomes through disciplined planning, prioritization, and cross-functional collaboration. | Features shipped, velocity, quality metrics, impact deltas (%/ $). |
| **Customer Insight** | Depth of understanding of user needs and market context; the foundation for sound product decisions. | Research synthesis, JTBD framing, TAM analysis, segmentation work. |
| **Strategy** | The capacity to translate insight into direction—defining problems, setting goals, sequencing bets, and aligning with business outcomes. | Roadmaps, PRDs with trade-offs, KPIs defined and hit, pivots justified by data. |
| **Influence** | The ability to align, persuade, and mobilize others without direct authority. | Stakeholder alignment, cross-team initiatives, exec/board-level communication. |

### The Hidden Dimension: Scope

Scope captures how widely a PM applies those competencies.

It scales from feature → product → portfolio → organization, and determines level progression:

| Level | Level Code | Typical Scope Indicators |
|-------|------------|-------------------------|
| **Associate Product Manager** | L3 | Owns single features; executes defined strategy. |
| **Product Manager** | L4 | Owns a product area; sets roadmap and KPIs. |
| **Senior Product Manager** | L5 | Owns a product line; leads strategic execution across teams. |
| **Staff/Principal Product Manager** | L6 | Owns multiple product lines; shapes org-level standards and direction. |
| **Group Product Manager** | M1 | Leads a product group; manages teams and organizational strategy. |
| **Director of Product** | M2 | Leads product organization; drives company-wide initiatives and business leadership. |

Scope isn't a separate skill—it's a multiplier on the four competencies, drawn directly from content evidence (team count, revenue/users impacted, breadth of ownership, and decision-making autonomy).

### IC vs. Manager Track

Our framework distinguishes between Individual Contributor (IC) and Manager tracks:

- **IC Track (L3-L6)**: Advancement through demonstrated competency and scope expansion
- **Manager Track (M1-M2)**: Advancement through team leadership, organizational design, and people management

The Manager track requires evidence of hiring, mentoring, and managing multiple teams, which shifts the evaluation criteria from pure product work to organizational leadership.

### Why We Exclude "Autonomy" and "Leadership" as Core Pillars

Autonomy is expressed through Strategy: who frames the problem and sets direction.

People Leadership appears only in the Manager track, where evidence shifts to hiring, mentoring, and organizational design.

This keeps the IC model simple, explainable, and directly tied to observable work.

### Role-Type Specializations

We support specialization overlays that highlight relevant evidence and match work to role archetypes:

| Role Type | Evidence Focus |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Growth** | Execution (experimentation, A/B testing), Strategy (hypothesis/metrics), user acquisition and retention patterns |
| **Platform** | Influence (infrastructure alignment, cross-team coordination), Execution (technical complexity, API design) |
| **AI/ML** | Strategy (model evaluation, algorithm design), Execution (data-driven product development) |
| **Founding Context** | Strategy & Influence (direction-setting, resource-constrained execution), reduced penalty for missing large-scale metrics |

These specializations don't reweight the core competency scores—they help match your work history to industry patterns and highlight where your experience aligns with specific PM archetypes. This preserves a single ladder while personalizing evaluation to each user's domain.

### TL;DR

Narrata measures Execution, Customer Insight, Strategy, and Influence, then scales them by Scope.

This structure mirrors how the industry actually levels PMs—simple enough for users to understand, rigorous enough for data-driven assessment.
